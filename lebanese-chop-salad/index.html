Lebanese Chop Salad

Ingredients

  • 1 cup chickpea (dried)
  • 3 cucumber
  • 4 Tbsp Za'atar
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp crushed and minced black pepper
  • 1/4 tea saffron
  • 2 small red onions in one cm cubes
  • 1 head garlic minced (~6 cloves)
  • Juice of 10 key limes
  • 3 hefty Tbsp olive oil
  1. Soak chickpeas in water overnight, rinse and wash out water.
  2. Cut ends of cucumber, then cut in half.
  3. Take half of the cucumber, lay flat and remove the seeded center by cutting each end.
  4. Cut cucumber ends into strips then into cubes.
  5. Add cucumber into large bowl. Combine with Za'atar, salt, black pepper and saffron. Stir.
  6. Combine chickpeas, chopped onions, minced garlic, lime juice and olive oil to mixture.

White Bean Sauce

  • 1 cup Great Northern Bean (dried)
  • 2 tea salt
  • 3 Tbsp tahini
  • 2 Tbsp olive oil

Combine all ingredients in a blender.

  1. Soak beans overnight in water.
  2. Drain and rinse beans and place in pot, cover with drinking water. Cook for 50 minutes or until soft. This water will not be discarded.
  3. Let beans cool down and blend beans and water mixture with salt, tahini and olive oil until smooth.

Lavash (serious eats - pita)

Follow these steps to make the first dough

Ingredients

  • 2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup wheat flour
  • 2 1/4 tea yeast (instant, not Rapid Rise, not Active Dry)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 Tbsp sugar
  • 1 tea salt
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil

Steps

  1. Add yeast to water, stir.
  2. Combine flour, salt, oil in large glass bowl.
  3. Mix flour, salt, oil and combine yeast and water with spatula.
  4. Knead for 1 minute with hands.
  5. When it's too dry, add more oil or water as needed.
  6. Continue to knead for a few minutes.
  7. Add 1 1/2 cup flour and add water continually.
  8. Knead more, should be smooth, not dry.
  9. Cover with saran wrap. Leave out overnight to let rise.
  10. The next day, preheat oven to 550 F.
  11. Take half the dough and leave aside, I put it in the fridge. This will become your starter dough for the next day. If you do not want to make more bread the next day, feel free to use all the dough for more bread.
  12. Use the other half and split into thirds.
  13. Take one third and flatten using a rolling pin, using flour to prevent the dough from sticking on the rolling pin and baking sheet to make flatbread.Place flattened dough on baking sheet and bake for 4 minutes.
  14. Repeat making as many flatbreads as you like.
